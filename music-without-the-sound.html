MUSIC WITHOUT THE SOUND

Sofia M.

According to The World Health Organization, over 430 million people suffer from some form of hearing loss. That leaves a huge majority of the world's population without issues, which is made clear in mainstream media. Individuals with hearing loss, or complete loss, have very little outlets to turn to that cater to their needs. Interpreters can be costly for individuals, especially when 80% of those suffering from disability hearing loss come from middle to low-income backgrounds.

The Impact of Music

 

Music is one of, if not the, most important creative outlets for humans. Music dates back to ancient times, and it's only taking up a larger portion of our lives as centuries pass. With musical acts getting larger than life, the growth of accessibility to music for the hearing impaired hasn't quite taken the turn one would expect. 

One thing that hit me especially hard this year was watching the Superbowl Half-Time Show with The Weeknd performing his top hits on one of the world's biggest stages. The movements from the R&B singer and his dancers were chaotic and mesmerizing when paired with his songs. But it made me wonder how interesting the show would be if I couldn't hear the music, I would be staring into chaos without that binding agent that is music.

Sign Language Music Videos

 

In a new project dedicated to making music more accessible to the hearing impaired, deaf actor and dancer Mervin Primeaux-O'Bryant and hearing dancer and choreographer Brandon Kazen-Maddox are revolutionizing music videos. The two have experience in making, and remaking, music videos that focus on connecting movement to music. It's not just dancing, they're visibly acting out and feeling the music. 

The duo is working on a ten part series of American Sign Language (ASL) covers of seminal works by black women. These music videos are signed, acted out, and danced to show the perfect rhythm that is the song. So many times hearing people forget that music is more than just words to the hearing impaired. Deafness can come later in life to some, which puts a lot of deaf people on the planet who very well know that emotion is fundamental to music and song understanding. 

While the project hasn't been released for me to share with you, here is a great example of  the power an ASL music video; done by Kazen-Maddox.

Here is another example, from Primeaux-O'Bryant. 

It's clear that the duo will make great art and videos together. This is a huge step for the hearing disabled community. The gap that Mervin and Brandon are attempting to bridge is one that would allow for deaf, or partially deaf, community members to enjoy music for the first time in years, or for the first time ever.
